# Executive summary: technology pricing analysis

We analysed 1,303 real laptop configurations to understand what drives their price and to build a model that predicts price from specifications.

The model explains roughly **77%** of the variation in price on laptops it has never seen, with a typical error of about **16%** (around $116). In plain terms, given a laptop's specs we can estimate its price closely enough to benchmark listings, flag mispriced products, and guide configuration decisions.

The biggest price levers are **RAM, CPU clock speed, and CPU tier**, alongside solid-state storage. Storage type alone is striking: laptops with an SSD command a median premium of about 125% over those without. The market also separates into three clean tiers, Budget, Mainstream, and Premium, that differ most in memory, display sharpness, and SSD adoption.

For a pricing team, this means the highest-leverage specs to model first are RAM, CPU, and SSD; the tier boundaries show where a configuration sits in the market and what spec change would move it; and the per-unit model can automatically flag products priced far from their predicted value.
